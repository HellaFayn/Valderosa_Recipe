"Pinakbet: A Flavorful Journey Through Filipino Cuisine"

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork ribs or pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 small bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 medium squash, cubed
  • 1 cup long beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup okra, halved lengthwise
  • 2 medium tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt, to taste

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and prepare all the vegetables. Slice the bitter melon, eggplant, and squash into bite-sized pieces. Cut the long beans into 2-inch segments and halve the okra lengthwise. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sliced onion, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add Protein (Optional): If using pork belly or shrimp, add them to the pan and cook until they are lightly browned.
  4. Incorporate Shrimp Paste: Add the shrimp paste (bagoong) to the pan and stir well to combine with the aromatics and protein. Let it cook for a minute to release its flavors.
  5. Add Vegetables: Begin adding the prepared vegetables to the pan, starting with the hardest ones. Add the bitter melon first, followed by the squash, eggplant, long beans, and okra. Arrange them evenly in the pan.
  6. Season to Taste: Taste the pinakbet and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add salt if necessary, bearing in mind that shrimp paste is already salty. Serve Hot: Once the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together, remove the pan from the heat. Transfer the pinakbet to a serving dish and serve hot alongside steamed rice.
