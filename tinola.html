"Tinola: A Taste of Filipino Comfort and Tradition"

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, julienned
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 2 medium green papayas, peeled, seeds removed, and sliced into wedges
  • 2-3 pieces chayote (sayote), peeled, seeds removed, and sliced into wedges
  • 8 cups water
  • 8 cups water
  • Ground black pepper, to taste

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Rinse the chicken pieces under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the julienned ginger and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the pot and sauté until they are lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Incorporate Onions: Add the sliced onion to the pot and continue to sauté until the onions are translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour Water: Pour the water into the pot, covering the chicken and aromatics. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer, covered, for about 20-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Add Vegetables: Once the chicken is cooked, add the sliced green papayas and chayote (sayote) to the pot. Let them simmer until the vegetables are tender but still slightly firm, about 5-7 minutes.
  7. Season to Taste: Season the tinola with fish sauce (patis) or salt and ground black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference. Tinola should have a balanced flavor of saltiness and slight sweetness from the vegetables.
  8. Serve Hot: Once all the vegetables are cooked and the flavors have melded together, remove the pot from the heat. Serve the tinola hot in individual bowls, alongside steamed rice.
